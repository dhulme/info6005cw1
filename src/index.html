US Spending

What does it show?

It shows the planned and actual US spending across the different government agencies, investments and projects.

How is it interactive?

Users can get a description of any wedge by hovering over it. They can click on any parent wedge to zoom in and see its children in more detail. Finally users can toggle at any time between planned and actual spending.

Who is it for?

The infographic is aimed at interested members of the public who want to quickly see how government money is spent and any variances in planned and actual spending.

Project Gantt Chart

What does it show?

Through a Gantt chart it shows the the allocation of time between the projects that comprise each investment.

How is it interactive?

By using the two drop down menus, users can show the Gantt chart for the exact investment they are looking for.

Who is it for?

Primarily aimed at managers within government agencies who would be able to see how long each project took.

US Spending Variance

What does it show?

It shows which agencies overspent and saved the most money, both as a percentage of their budgets and in real terms.

How is it interactive?

Users can change between seeing the percentage and cash values.

Who is it for?

Aimed at members of the public who want to quickly see which government agencies are overspending.
