US Spending

What does it show?

It shows the planned and actual US spending across the different government agencies, investments and projects in a sunburst layout with increasing detail in each layer. It is an appropriate way to show the data because each child node contributes to their parent node, finally contributing to overall spending.

Who is it for?

The visualisation is aimed at members of the public who want to see how government money is spent and variances in planned and actual spending. It attempts to give users a high level understanding of US spending very quickly through the central ring whilst providing a greater detail for interested users through the outer rings.

How is it interactive?

Users can get a description of any wedge by hovering over it. They can click on any parent wedge to zoom in and see its children in more detail. Finally they can toggle at any time between planned and actual spending.

The ability to zoom enables interested users to learn more and is appropriate because there are many nodes in the dataset.

Toggling between planned and actual spending is useful for users as it allows them to see which areas have overspent and thus hold the US government to account. These categories are appropriate as they correspond to the two distinct financial categories in the dataset.

Project Gantt Chart

What does it show?

Through a Gantt chart it shows the the start and end dates of the projects that comprise each investment. In addition it shows the project duration and which projects happened concurrently. Gantt charts are a common way to show temporal project data.

Who is it for?

Primarily aimed at managers within government agencies who would be interested in how long each project took and how the projects overlapped. The visualisation is appropriate for this audience as they would be familiar with Gantt charts and it communicates the data effectively.

How is it interactive?

By using the two drop down menus, users can display the Gantt chart for the exact investment they are looking for. This interaction is appropriate to the way the data is structured and appropriate for the audience who know the exact investment they want to display.

US Spending Variance

What does it show?

It shows which agencies overspent and saved the most money, both as a percentage of their budgets and in real terms.

How is it interactive?

Users can change between seeing the percentage and cash values.

Who is it for?

Aimed at members of the public who want to quickly see which government agencies are overspending.
